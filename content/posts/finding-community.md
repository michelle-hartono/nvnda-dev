## The Outsider Phase

For a long time, I lurked. I read writeups, watched conference talks, followed researchers on Twitter — but never felt like I belonged. Security felt like a club with an invisible entrance exam I hadn't studied for.

## What Changed

A CTF. A Discord server. A few people who answered my "dumb" questions without making me feel dumb.

That's really it. The barrier wasn't skill — it was just finding the right room. Once I was in a space where curiosity was celebrated over credentials, everything clicked.

## What I've Learned About Community

- **Teach what you know, even if it feels basic.** Someone else is where you were six months ago.
- **Ask questions publicly.** The "stupid" question you're embarrassed to ask is probably the one fifteen other people have.
- **Show your work.** Imperfect writeups still have value. Ship them.

## Going Forward

I'm trying to be more intentional about giving back — answering questions in community channels, writing more here, mentoring when I can.

If you're in that outsider phase right now: you're not alone, and you're closer than you think.
